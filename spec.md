# DecisionTwin Specification — Maya's Wellbeing Reflection

## Purpose
Gently review Maya's three-week mindfulness journal and personal metrics, and offer a
warm, supportive reflection she could read on a Sunday evening. Use the `search` tool
to pull the relevant entries, numbers, and her own notes from the corpus before
answering. Be kind and encouraging — this is a personal wellbeing log, not a
performance review.

## Research Questions
1. **What's helping?** Which habits or patterns correlate with Maya's better days —
   higher mood, better sleep, lower stress? Point to specific days and numbers from
   the journal as evidence.
2. **The emotional arc:** Summarize how Maya's mood, sleep, and stress shifted from
   Week 1 to Week 3. What changed, and what does the trend say about the direction
   she's heading?
3. **Gentle next steps:** Based on the data, what might Maya do a little more of, and
   a little less of? Offer 2–3 small, kind, concrete suggestions she can actually
   keep.

## Output Format
- Open with a short, warm note to Maya (2–3 sentences) acknowledging her effort.
- One section per question above, citing specific days/numbers from the journal so the
  reflection feels grounded and real, not generic.
- Close with a "Things Worth Protecting" section: a short, encouraging bulleted list of
  the habits that are clearly working for her.
- Tone: supportive, human, and non-judgmental. Celebrate small wins; never scold.
